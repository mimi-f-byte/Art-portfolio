About

Working in two lights

My studio holds two ongoing conversations. Luminous is where I chase weather — the particular gold of late afternoon, fog breaking over water, the softness of a room lit by one window. It's built in oil and watercolor, layered slowly, in the impressionist tradition of painting light itself rather than the object it falls on.

Nocturne is the other half: bolder, faster, built in acrylic and ink, closer to animation than observation. These are the pieces that come out after the sun goes down — more graphic, more invented, more willing to break a rule the daylight work wouldn't.

I trained across traditional and contemporary methods and now move between them depending on what a piece is asking for. Every work you see here, in either body, starts on paper before it starts on canvas.
